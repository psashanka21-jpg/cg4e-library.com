The Two Goats

$2.00

"The Two Goats" is a short fable that teaches a lesson about arrogance and stubbornness. The story takes place in a mountainous region where a narrow bridge crosses a deep stream. One day, two goats approach the bridge from opposite directions. Since the bridge is too narrow for both to pass at the same time, they face a dilemma. Instead of compromising or taking turns, both goats insist on their right to cross first. Neither wants to back down, and they engage in a struggle to push the other off. As a result, they both lose their balance and fall into the stream below. The moral of the story is that stubbornness and pride can lead to unnecessary conflicts and negative consequences. It emphasizes the importance of compromise, patience, and cooperation in resolving disputes.

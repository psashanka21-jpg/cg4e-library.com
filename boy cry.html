About the Author

Aesop's Fables is a collection of short moral tales attributed to Aesop, a storyteller believed to have lived in ancient Greece around 620â€“564 BCE. These fables feature animals, plants, and inanimate objects that act and speak like humans, using simple narratives to convey universal moral lessons. Though little is known about Aesop's life, he is often described as a slave who gained freedom through his wit and storytelling ability. The fables were originally passed down orally before being written and compiled over centuries by various authors and translators, including Phaedrus and Babrius. The stories, such as The Tortoise and the Hare, The Fox and the Grapes, and The Boy Who Cried Wolf, emphasize values like honesty, humility, and perseverance. They remain culturally significant, influencing literature, education, and art worldwide, and are often used to teach ethics and critical thinking.

Ant and the Grasshopper

The Ant and the Grasshopper is a famous fable by Aesop that teaches the value of hard work and preparation. The story follows an ant and a grasshopper who have very different approaches to life. During the warm summer, the ant works tirelessly, gathering food and storing it for the winter. Meanwhile, the grasshopper spends his days singing, dancing, and enjoying himself, mocking the ant for working so hard instead of having fun. However, when winter arrives, the grasshopper finds himself cold and hungry, with no food to survive. He goes to the ant and begs for help, but the ant reminds him that he had all summer to prepare. In some versions of the fable, the ant refuses to help, teaching the grasshopper a harsh lesson about responsibility, while in others, the ant shares but warns him to be wiser in the future.

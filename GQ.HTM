About Magazine

GQ (Gentlemen's Quarterly) is a globally renowned men's magazine that began in 1931 as Apparel Arts, a trade publication for men's fashion. Relaunched as Gentlemen's Quarterly in 1957, it was later renamed GQ in 1967. Published by Cond√© Nast, GQ is celebrated for its cutting-edge content on men's fashion, style, grooming, culture, and lifestyle. The magazine combines high-end fashion editorial with compelling journalism, covering topics like entertainment, politics, fitness, technology, and relationships. GQ has a reputation for featuring prominent celebrities, athletes, and cultural icons on its covers, reflecting its appeal to a stylish, modern audience. Known for its influential annual awards, such as the "Men of the Year" issue, GQ has become a cultural benchmark for contemporary masculinity, blending sophistication with bold storytelling. Its international editions make it a global authority on men's style and culture.

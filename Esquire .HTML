About Magazine

Esquire is a leading men's lifestyle magazine that debuted in 1933, founded by Arnold Gingrich, David A. Smart, and Henry L. Jackson. Initially focused on providing advice and entertainment for men during the Great Depression, it quickly evolved into a sophisticated publication that merged style with substance. The magazine is renowned for its high-quality journalism, featuring essays, investigative pieces, and interviews by acclaimed writers like Ernest Hemingway, F. Scott Fitzgerald, and Gay Talese. Over time, it expanded its content to include fashion, grooming, culture, politics, health, technology, and entertainment. Esquire is particularly famous for its striking cover designs and iconic "Esquire Man" persona, which embodies a blend of modern masculinity, refinement, and curiosity. It often features celebrities, thought leaders, and cultural icons, making it a staple for readers seeking insightful commentary and timeless style.
